  • Negros Oriental

    • ╰┈➤   Negros Oriental is a province in the Philippines located in what is now called the Negros Island Region. Located in the Visayas, the region is home to 1.4 million people, who speak mainly Bisaya/Cebuano. It neighbors Cebu and Siquijor, and shares a lot of wonderful sights with them.

    Dumaguete City
    »    In what is dubbed "The City of Gentle People" is a bounty of places for both local and foreign tourists seeking an adventure. Dumaguete is the Region's capital and home of Silliman University, the alma mater of this website's creator.

    Manjuyod White Sand Bar
    »    58 kilometers from Dumaguete, Manjuyod City prides itself on having a white sand bar flocked to by many. With the crystal clear water and a thrilling kayak ride to some awesome sights and native houses, you will surely be lulled and charmed by the island. Be on the lookout for the cute and cool dolphins, too!

    Dauin
    »    Rich in aquatic culture and biodiversity, Dauin is known by many locals to be the place to go for scuba diving and snorkling. Coming here from Dumaguete takes a short 15-kilometer trip, and you can visit the Apo Island while you're here.



  • Cebu

    • ╰┈➤   Cebu City is another destination superstar in Region 7. Visitors come to visit the White Beach in Moalboal, spend time with the Whale Sharks in Oslob, or take a plunge in Kawasan Falls (it's extremely cold here, by the way.), or appreciate love, time, and history at Magellan's Cross and the Temple of Leah.



  • Bohol

    • ╰┈➤   Of course, who could forget the tarsiers of Bohol? These little beings are the pride and joy of the municipality, along with the Chocolate Hills, one of the Seven Wonders of the World (please do note that tarsiers are not to be disturbed, as they are nocturnal and are easily stressed out). Bohol also has other sites such as the Bohol Island and the Loboc Floating Resto Cruise.
